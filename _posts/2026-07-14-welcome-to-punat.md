Punat is a small, sheltered harbour town on the eastern shore of the island of Krk,
Croatia. The bay is calm and crystal-clear, ringed by pine-covered hillsides and
the white stone walls of the old village.

## First impressions

Arriving by ferry feels like stepping into a postcard. The promenade is lined with
fig trees, and the smell of salt and rosemary fills the air. Children swim off the
old stone pier while fishing boats bob gently alongside the larger yachts in the
marina.

## Things to do

- Walk to the Franciscan monastery on the island of Košljun — a five-minute boat
  ride from the harbour.
- Hire a bicycle and ride the coastal path toward Krk town.
- Eat grilled fish at one of the konobas along the waterfront.
- Watch the sunset from the hill above the village.
